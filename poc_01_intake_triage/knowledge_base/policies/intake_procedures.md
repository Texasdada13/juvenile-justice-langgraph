# Juvenile Intake Procedures Manual

## Section 1: Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose
This manual establishes standardized procedures for the intake process when a youth is referred to the juvenile justice system. Proper intake procedures ensure:
- Youth rights are protected
- Information is accurately gathered
- Appropriate decisions are made
- Services are properly matched

### 1.2 Scope
These procedures apply to all intake officers and staff processing juvenile referrals from:
- Law enforcement agencies
- Schools
- Parents/guardians
- Other agencies
- Self-referrals

## Section 2: Initial Contact

### 2.1 Receiving the Referral
Upon receiving a referral:
1. Log referral in case management system
2. Assign case number
3. Verify youth's identity and age
4. Confirm jurisdiction
5. Document referring party information

### 2.2 Required Information from Referral Source
- Youth's full name and date of birth
- Parent/guardian contact information
- Nature of alleged offense
- Date, time, and location of offense
- Victim information (if applicable)
- Witness information
- Youth's current location
- Any immediate safety concerns

### 2.3 Priority Referrals
Expedite intake for:
- Youth currently in custody
- Violent offenses
- Youth experiencing mental health crisis
- Victim safety concerns
- Sex offenses

## Section 3: Youth Interview

### 3.1 Before the Interview
- Verify parent/guardian notification
- Arrange interpreter if needed
- Review any prior case history
- Prepare interview space (private, comfortable)
- Gather necessary forms

### 3.2 Rights Notification
Before any questioning:
1. Inform youth of the nature of the referral
2. Advise of right to remain silent
3. Advise of right to attorney
4. If under 15: Parent/guardian MUST be present
5. Document rights notification

### 3.3 Interview Topics
Cover the following areas:

**A. Demographic Information**
- Current address and housing stability
- School enrollment and attendance
- Employment (if applicable)
- Emergency contacts

**B. Family Situation**
- Household composition
- Parent/guardian involvement
- Family stressors
- Support systems available

**C. Education**
- Current school and grade
- Academic performance
- Attendance issues
- Special education status (IEP/504)
- Disciplinary history

**D. Peer Relationships**
- Friend groups
- Gang involvement (if any)
- Prosocial activities
- Negative peer influences

**E. Substance Use**
- Alcohol use history
- Drug use history
- Current use patterns
- Treatment history

**F. Mental Health**
- Prior diagnoses
- Current symptoms
- Treatment history
- Medications
- Trauma history

**G. Prior Legal History**
- Prior referrals
- Prior adjudications
- Current supervision status
- Pending cases

**H. Current Offense**
- Youth's account of events
- Level of responsibility accepted
- Impact awareness
- Victim relationship

**I. Strengths and Protective Factors**
- Talents and interests
- Positive relationships
- Goals and aspirations
- Resilience factors

### 3.4 Interview Best Practices
- Use age-appropriate language
- Build rapport before sensitive topics
- Allow youth to tell their story
- Avoid leading questions
- Take notes, but maintain eye contact
- Be patient and non-judgmental

## Section 4: Parent/Guardian Interview

### 4.1 Required Contact
Parent/guardian must be contacted within 2 hours of intake initiation.

### 4.2 Information to Gather
- Verification of youth information
- Family history
- Youth's behavioral history
- Prior services received
- Parental concerns
- Supervision capabilities
- Willingness to participate in services

### 4.3 Information to Provide
- Nature of referral
- Intake process explanation
- Potential outcomes
- Rights of youth and family
- Next steps and timeline
- Contact information for questions

## Section 5: Assessment and Screening

### 5.1 Required Assessments
All intakes must include:
1. Risk Assessment Instrument (RAI)
2. Mental Health Screening (MAYSI-2 or equivalent)
3. Substance Abuse Screening
4. Educational needs screen
5. Trauma screen (when indicated)

### 5.2 When Additional Assessment Needed
Refer for comprehensive assessment if:
- Positive mental health screen
- Substance dependence indicated
- Developmental concerns
- Complex trauma history
- Prior treatment failures

## Section 6: Decision Points

### 6.1 Case Disposition Options
After completing intake, determine:

**A. Divert/Dismiss**
- Insufficient evidence
- Not in interest of justice
- Better handled informally

**B. Informal Adjustment**
- First-time, minor offense
- Youth accepts responsibility
- Family willing to participate
- Victim agrees (if applicable)

**C. Diversion Program**
- Meets diversion eligibility
- Appropriate program available
- Youth/family consent obtained

**D. Petition Filing**
- Serious offense
- Prior history
- Public safety concerns
- Diversion inappropriate

### 6.2 Detention Decision
If youth in custody, determine within 6 hours:
- Release to parent/guardian
- Release to alternative caregiver
- Release with conditions
- Detention pending hearing

Use RAI score and detention criteria policy.

### 6.3 Required Documentation
Document in case file:
- Disposition decision
- Reasoning for decision
- Alternatives considered
- RAI score and level
- Recommendations
- Next steps

## Section 7: Special Circumstances

### 7.1 Youth Experiencing Mental Health Crisis
1. Ensure immediate safety
2. Contact mental health crisis team
3. Do not detain solely for mental health
4. Arrange appropriate evaluation
5. Document crisis response

### 7.2 Suspected Child Abuse/Neglect
1. Report to Child Protective Services
2. Document concerns
3. Coordinate with CPS investigation
4. Consider safety planning
5. Do not release to suspected abuser

### 7.3 Youth as Victim
If youth discloses victimization:
1. Take disclosure seriously
2. Report as required by law
3. Offer victim services
4. Consider impact on current case
5. Document appropriately

### 7.4 Immigration Concerns
1. Do not inquire about immigration status
2. Immigration status does not affect processing
3. Provide interpretation services
4. Know rights of immigrant youth
5. Connect with legal resources if requested

## Section 8: Documentation Standards

### 8.1 Required Documentation
Complete within 24 hours of intake:
- Intake report form
- RAI score sheet
- Screening results
- Interview notes
- Decision summary
- Service referrals

### 8.2 Quality Standards
All documentation must be:
- Accurate and factual
- Objective (avoid opinions)
- Complete
- Legible
- Properly signed/dated
- Entered in case management system

### 8.3 Confidentiality
- Records are confidential
- Share only with authorized parties
- Follow FERPA for education records
- Follow HIPAA for health information
- Document all disclosures

## Section 9: Timelines

### 9.1 Required Timelines
| Action | Timeline |
|--------|----------|
| Parent notification | Within 2 hours |
| Detention decision (if in custody) | Within 6 hours |
| Detention hearing | Within 24 hours (excl. weekends) |
| Intake completion | Within 5 business days |
| Diversion decision | Within 10 business days |
| Petition filing decision | Within 30 days |

### 9.2 Extensions
Extensions require supervisor approval and documentation.

## Section 10: Quality Assurance

### 10.1 Supervisor Review
Supervisors shall review:
- All detention decisions
- All petition filing recommendations
- 20% of diversion decisions
- All cases involving special populations

### 10.2 Performance Metrics
Track and report:
- Time to complete intake
- RAI inter-rater reliability
- Disposition outcomes
- Diversion completion rates
- Recidivism rates by disposition
