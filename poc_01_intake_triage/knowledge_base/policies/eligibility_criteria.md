# Program Eligibility Criteria Manual

## Section 1: Overview

### 1.1 Purpose
This manual defines eligibility criteria for all juvenile justice programs and services. Proper determination ensures youth are matched with appropriate interventions.

### 1.2 Programs Covered
- Diversion Programs
- Teen Court
- Restorative Justice
- Family Counseling
- Substance Abuse Treatment
- Mental Health Services
- Community Service
- Electronic Monitoring
- Intensive Supervision

## Section 2: Diversion Program Eligibility

### 2.1 Standard Diversion
**Eligible if ALL criteria met:**
- First-time offender (no prior adjudications)
- Current offense is misdemeanor
- No offense against person
- Youth admits responsibility
- Parent/guardian agrees to participate
- Victim agrees (if applicable)

**Ineligible if ANY present:**
- Prior adjudication for any offense
- Current felony charge
- Sex offense
- Firearm offense
- DUI/DWI

### 2.2 Enhanced Diversion
**Eligible if ALL criteria met:**
- No more than one prior adjudication (non-violent)
- Current offense is non-violent felony or repeat misdemeanor
- Risk assessment score: Low-Moderate or below
- Stable living situation
- Not currently on probation for felony

## Section 3: Teen Court Eligibility

### 3.1 Criteria
**Eligible if ALL criteria met:**
- Age 12-17
- First-time offender
- Misdemeanor offense only
- Admits to offense
- Parent/guardian consent obtained
- Not a status offense (separate track)

**Best suited for:**
- Shoplifting (under $500)
- Minor vandalism
- Disorderly conduct
- Minor in possession (first offense)
- Simple assault (no injury)

### 3.2 Exclusions
- Offenses involving weapons
- Drug distribution
- Sexual offenses
- Offenses with significant victim injury
- Gang-related offenses

## Section 4: Restorative Justice Programs

### 4.1 Victim-Offender Mediation
**Eligible if ALL criteria met:**
- Identifiable victim willing to participate
- Youth accepts responsibility
- Offense appropriate for mediation
- Youth capable of meaningful participation
- No active protective order

**Preferred offenses:**
- Property crimes
- Minor assaults
- Harassment
- Theft

### 4.2 Community Conferencing
**Eligible if:**
- Youth accepts responsibility
- Community impact identified
- Support persons available for youth
- Facilitator determines appropriateness

## Section 5: Treatment Programs

### 5.1 Substance Abuse Treatment
**Eligible if:**
- Identified substance use issue
- Current offense is substance-related OR
- Assessment indicates treatment need
- Youth willing to participate (or court-ordered)

**Levels of Care:**
| Level | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| Education/Prevention | First-time use, no dependence |
| Outpatient | Mild to moderate use disorder |
| Intensive Outpatient | Moderate use disorder, failed outpatient |
| Residential | Severe use disorder, safety concerns |

### 5.2 Mental Health Services
**Eligible if:**
- Mental health screening indicates need
- Diagnosed mental health condition OR
- Behavioral indicators warrant assessment
- Services not contraindicated

**Priority indicators:**
- History of trauma
- Self-harm or suicidal ideation
- Psychotic symptoms
- Severe anxiety/depression
- Conduct disorder symptoms

## Section 6: Community Supervision Programs

### 6.1 Standard Probation
**Assigned when:**
- Youth adjudicated delinquent
- Offense warrants supervision but not placement
- Community safety can be maintained
- Family able to provide housing

### 6.2 Intensive Supervision Program (ISP)
**Eligible if:**
- Higher risk youth who would otherwise be placed
- Risk score: Moderate-High or High
- Prior probation failures
- Need for enhanced structure
- Community safety concerns manageable

**Requirements:**
- Multiple weekly contacts
- Electronic monitoring capability
- Curfew compliance
- School/work verification

### 6.3 Electronic Monitoring
**Eligible if:**
- Alternative to detention needed
- Stable residence with electricity
- Responsible adult in home
- Youth agrees to conditions
- Not charged with violent offense requiring detention

## Section 7: Age-Specific Considerations

### 7.1 Youth Under 12
- Diversion strongly preferred
- Detention only for violent felony with court approval
- Family-based interventions prioritized
- Developmental assessment required

### 7.2 Youth 12-14
- Diversion preferred for first offense
- Consider developmental maturity
- School-based interventions when possible
- Family involvement required

### 7.3 Youth 15-17
- Full range of programs available
- Consider employment/education status
- Transition planning for youth approaching 18
- Adult court waiver considerations for serious offenses

## Section 8: Special Populations

### 8.1 Youth with Disabilities
- All programs must provide reasonable accommodations
- Eligibility cannot be denied solely due to disability
- Specialized programming may be required
- IEP/504 plan review required

### 8.2 Youth with Immigration Concerns
- Immigration status does NOT affect program eligibility
- Confidentiality protections apply
- Coordination with immigration counsel when needed
- Language access required

### 8.3 LGBTQ+ Youth
- Non-discrimination in all placements
- Housing placement based on youth's expressed gender
- Confidentiality regarding identity
- Specialized support services available

### 8.4 Pregnant/Parenting Youth
- Prenatal care access required
- Parenting classes available
- Child welfare coordination when needed
- Modified supervision schedules

## Section 9: Eligibility Determination Process

### 9.1 Required Steps
1. Complete intake assessment
2. Administer risk assessment instrument
3. Review offense information
4. Assess family/support system
5. Identify special needs
6. Match to eligible programs
7. Document determination

### 9.2 Documentation Requirements
- Eligibility checklist for each program considered
- Reason for program recommendation
- Reason for rejection of alternatives
- Youth/family preferences noted
- Supervisor review for high-risk cases

### 9.3 Appeals Process
Youth/family may appeal eligibility determination:
1. Written request within 5 business days
2. Review by supervisor not involved in original decision
3. Decision within 10 business days
4. Further appeal to program director if denied
