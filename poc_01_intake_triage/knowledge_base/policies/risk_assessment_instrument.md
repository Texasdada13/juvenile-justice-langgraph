# Risk Assessment Instrument (RAI) Manual

## Section 1: Purpose and Overview

### 1.1 Purpose
This Risk Assessment Instrument (RAI) provides an objective, validated tool for assessing the likelihood that a youth will:
1. Fail to appear for court proceedings
2. Commit a new offense while awaiting adjudication

### 1.2 When to Administer
The RAI shall be completed:
- At initial intake for all youth referred to the juvenile justice system
- Before any detention decision is made
- When circumstances significantly change

## Section 2: Scoring Instructions

### 2.1 General Guidelines
- Score based on verified information only
- Use official records when available
- Interview youth and guardian to gather information
- Document source of information for each item

### 2.2 Risk Factor Categories

#### Category A: Current Offense (0-6 points)
| Factor | Points |
|--------|--------|
| Most serious current charge is a status offense | 0 |
| Most serious current charge is a misdemeanor property offense | 1 |
| Most serious current charge is a misdemeanor person offense | 2 |
| Most serious current charge is a felony property offense | 3 |
| Most serious current charge is a felony drug offense | 4 |
| Most serious current charge is a felony person offense | 5 |
| Offense involved use of weapon | +1 |

#### Category B: Prior History (0-8 points)
| Factor | Points |
|--------|--------|
| No prior referrals | 0 |
| 1-2 prior referrals, no adjudications | 1 |
| 1-2 prior adjudications | 2 |
| 3+ prior adjudications | 4 |
| Prior felony adjudication | +2 |
| Prior violent offense adjudication | +2 |

#### Category C: Pending Cases/Supervision Status (0-4 points)
| Factor | Points |
|--------|--------|
| No pending cases, not on supervision | 0 |
| Pending case(s) | 2 |
| Currently on probation | 2 |
| Currently on parole/aftercare | 3 |
| Absconded from supervision | 4 |

#### Category D: History of Failure to Appear (0-4 points)
| Factor | Points |
|--------|--------|
| No prior FTAs | 0 |
| 1 FTA more than 12 months ago | 1 |
| 1 FTA within past 12 months | 2 |
| 2+ FTAs within past 12 months | 4 |

#### Category E: Living Situation (0-3 points)
| Factor | Points |
|--------|--------|
| Lives with parent/guardian, stable housing | 0 |
| Lives with relative, stable housing | 1 |
| Unstable housing situation | 2 |
| No parent/guardian available, runaway, homeless | 3 |

## Section 3: Scoring Matrix

### 3.1 Total Score Calculation
Add points from all categories (A through E).
Maximum possible score: 25 points

### 3.2 Risk Level Classification
| Score Range | Risk Level | Recommended Action |
|-------------|------------|-------------------|
| 0-5 | Low | Release to parent/guardian |
| 6-10 | Low-Moderate | Release with conditions |
| 11-15 | Moderate | Consider detention alternatives |
| 16-20 | Moderate-High | Detention may be appropriate |
| 21-25 | High | Detention likely appropriate |

## Section 4: Mandatory Overrides

### 4.1 Automatic Override to Detention
The following circumstances require automatic consideration for detention regardless of RAI score:
- Murder or attempted murder charge
- Sexual assault charge (1st degree)
- Use of firearm in commission of felony
- Escape from secure detention within past 30 days

### 4.2 Automatic Override to Release
The following circumstances require release regardless of RAI score:
- Youth is under 12 years old (unless violent felony)
- Charge is status offense only
- Medical condition requiring care unavailable in detention

## Section 5: Discretionary Overrides

### 5.1 Override Authority
Supervisors may approve overrides when documented justification exists.

### 5.2 Override Reasons - More Restrictive
- Credible threat to victim or witness
- Gang-related offense with identified risk
- Pattern of escalating behavior not captured in score

### 5.3 Override Reasons - Less Restrictive
- Strong community ties not captured in score
- Available intensive supervision program
- Mental health needs better served in community
- Very young age (12-13) with family support

## Section 6: Documentation Requirements

### 6.1 Required Documentation
Each RAI assessment must include:
- Date and time of assessment
- Name of assessor
- Source of information for each factor
- Total score and risk level
- Recommendation
- If override: supervisor approval and written justification

### 6.2 Quality Assurance
- 10% of all RAIs shall be audited monthly
- Inter-rater reliability checks conducted quarterly
- Annual validation study required

## Section 7: Protective Factors Assessment

### 7.1 Protective Factors (May Support Release)
While not scored in RAI, the following should be documented:
- Strong parent/guardian engagement
- Enrolled in school with good attendance
- Employment
- Participation in prosocial activities
- Connection to positive mentor/adult
- No substance abuse issues
- Stable mental health
- Expressed remorse/accountability

### 7.2 Using Protective Factors
Protective factors may support:
- Selection of less restrictive alternative
- Discretionary override to lower supervision level
- Case planning and service recommendations
