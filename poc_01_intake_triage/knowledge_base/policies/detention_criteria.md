# Juvenile Detention Criteria Policy

## Section 1: Purpose

### 1.1 Purpose Statement
This policy establishes criteria for the secure detention of juveniles pending court proceedings, consistent with the Juvenile Justice and Delinquency Prevention Act (JJDPA) and state law requirements.

### 1.2 Guiding Principles
- Detention shall be used only when necessary to protect public safety or ensure court appearance
- The least restrictive alternative shall always be considered
- No youth shall be detained solely due to lack of appropriate placement or services

## Section 2: Legal Standards

### 2.1 Burden of Proof
Detention requires clear and convincing evidence that:
1. The youth poses a substantial risk of harm to others, OR
2. The youth poses a substantial flight risk and is unlikely to appear for court

### 2.2 JJDPA Compliance
All detention decisions must comply with JJDPA core requirements:
- Deinstitutionalization of Status Offenders (DSO): Status offenders may NOT be detained
- Jail Removal: Juveniles may not be detained in adult jails (6-hour exception for processing)
- Sight and Sound Separation: Juveniles must be separated from adult inmates

## Section 3: Detention Criteria

### 3.1 Mandatory Detention Review
The following offenses require detention screening but do not mandate detention:
- Felony offenses against persons
- Offenses involving firearms
- Offenses committed while on probation or pending adjudication

### 3.2 Factors Supporting Detention
The following factors weigh in favor of detention:
- Violent offense with identified victim
- Use of weapon during offense
- History of failure to appear (2+ FTAs in past year)
- Current probation or parole status
- High risk assessment score
- Lack of responsible adult supervision
- Threats to victim or witnesses

### 3.3 Factors Against Detention
The following factors weigh against detention:
- First-time offender
- Non-violent offense
- Low or moderate risk assessment score
- Strong family support and supervision
- Enrolled in school with no major issues
- No prior failures to appear
- Stable housing situation

### 3.4 Status Offenses - NO DETENTION
Youth charged ONLY with status offenses may NOT be detained under any circumstances:
- Truancy
- Runaway
- Curfew violations
- Underage alcohol possession (if sole charge)
- Incorrigibility/ungovernability

## Section 4: Detention Alternatives

### 4.1 Required Consideration
Before ordering detention, the court or intake officer MUST consider:
1. Release to parent/guardian
2. Release to responsible adult
3. Electronic monitoring/GPS
4. Shelter care (non-secure)
5. Intensive supervision program
6. Day reporting

### 4.2 Documentation Requirement
If detention alternatives are rejected, the reason must be documented in writing.

## Section 5: Risk Assessment Instrument (RAI)

### 5.1 Required Use
All detention decisions shall be informed by the validated Risk Assessment Instrument (RAI).

### 5.2 RAI Scoring
| Score Range | Risk Level | Recommendation |
|-------------|------------|----------------|
| 0-5 | Low | Release |
| 6-10 | Low-Moderate | Release with conditions |
| 11-15 | Moderate | Consider alternatives |
| 16-20 | Moderate-High | Detention may be appropriate |
| 21+ | High | Detention likely appropriate |

### 5.3 Override Authority
RAI recommendations may be overridden with supervisor approval and documented justification.

## Section 6: Special Populations

### 6.1 Youth with Mental Health Needs
Youth experiencing mental health crisis shall be referred for mental health evaluation before detention decision.

### 6.2 Youth with Disabilities
Reasonable accommodations shall be made; detention facility must be able to meet youth's needs.

### 6.3 Immigration Status
Immigration status shall NOT be a factor in detention decisions.

## Section 7: Detention Review

### 7.1 Initial Hearing
Youth must appear before a judge within 24 hours of detention (excluding weekends/holidays).

### 7.2 Continued Detention Review
Detained youth shall have detention reviewed every 7 days.

### 7.3 Maximum Detention Period
Pre-adjudication detention shall not exceed 30 days without exceptional circumstances documented by the court.

## Section 8: Documentation Requirements

### 8.1 Required Documentation
All detention decisions must include:
- Completed RAI score sheet
- Written justification for detention
- Alternatives considered and reasons rejected
- Youth's risk level classification
- Victim safety considerations (if applicable)
