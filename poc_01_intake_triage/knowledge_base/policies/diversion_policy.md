# Youth Diversion Program Policy Manual

## Section 1: Purpose and Authority

### 1.1 Purpose
The Youth Diversion Program provides an alternative to formal court processing for eligible youth, offering intervention services that address underlying issues while holding youth accountable for their actions.

### 1.2 Legal Authority
This program operates under State Juvenile Code Section 42-101 et seq., which authorizes diversion of eligible youth from formal court processing.

## Section 2: Program Goals

1. Reduce recidivism through early intervention
2. Address root causes of delinquent behavior
3. Minimize unnecessary court involvement
4. Connect youth and families with community resources
5. Hold youth accountable while promoting rehabilitation

## Section 3: Eligibility Criteria

### 3.1 Age Requirements
Youth must be between 12 and 17 years of age at the time of the offense.

### 3.2 Offense Eligibility
Eligible offenses include:
- Misdemeanor offenses (excluding domestic violence)
- Low-level felony offenses (property crimes under $500)
- Status offenses (truancy, runaway, curfew violations)

### 3.3 Offense Exclusions
The following offenses are NOT eligible for diversion:
- Sexual offenses of any kind
- Offenses involving firearms or deadly weapons
- Gang-related violent offenses
- Offenses resulting in serious bodily injury
- Drug trafficking or distribution

### 3.4 Prior Record Requirements
- First-time offenders: Automatically eligible (if offense qualifies)
- Second-time offenders: Eligible with supervisor approval
- Three or more prior referrals: Generally ineligible; exceptions require director approval

### 3.5 Risk Level Requirements
- Low risk youth: Eligible
- Moderate risk youth: Eligible with enhanced services
- High risk youth: Generally ineligible; case-by-case consideration

### 3.6 Additional Requirements
- Youth must admit responsibility for the offense
- Parent/guardian must consent to participation
- Victim consent required for victim-offender mediation component
- Youth must reside within the county

## Section 4: Referral Process

### 4.1 Referral Sources
Referrals may be made by:
- Law enforcement officers
- School resource officers
- Prosecutors
- Juvenile court intake officers

### 4.2 Intake Assessment
All referred youth shall receive:
- Standardized risk/needs assessment
- Mental health screening
- Substance abuse screening
- Educational assessment review

## Section 5: Program Components

### 5.1 Required Components
All diversion participants must complete:
- Intake interview with youth and family
- Development of individualized service plan
- Community service hours (8-40 hours based on offense)
- Restitution (if applicable)

### 5.2 Optional Components (Based on Assessment)
- Cognitive-behavioral skills group
- Substance abuse education/treatment
- Mental health counseling
- Family therapy
- Tutoring/educational support
- Mentoring

## Section 6: Program Duration

### 6.1 Standard Duration
- Minimum: 90 days
- Maximum: 180 days
- Extensions may be granted for good cause

### 6.2 Successful Completion
Youth successfully complete the program when:
- All required components are completed
- No new offenses during program participation
- Restitution paid in full (if applicable)

## Section 7: Unsuccessful Termination

### 7.1 Grounds for Termination
- New delinquent offense
- Failure to comply with program requirements
- Failure to attend required sessions (3+ unexcused absences)
- Parent/guardian withdrawal of consent

### 7.2 Consequences
Upon unsuccessful termination, the case shall be referred to the prosecutor for formal processing.

## Section 8: Confidentiality

### 8.1 Record Keeping
Diversion records are confidential and shall be maintained separately from court records.

### 8.2 Record Expungement
Upon successful completion, diversion records may be expunged after 2 years with no subsequent offenses.
